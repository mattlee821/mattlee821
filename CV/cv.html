Research experience

I use molecular and genetic data to investigate risks and mechanisms of cancer development. Current work is focussed on integrative multi-omic analyses to identify networks and signatures of cancer susceptibility and resilience. I have experience working with large-scale resources (e.g., UK Biobank, EPIC) and deeply phenotyped cohorts (e.g., ALSPAC) across diverse study designs (e.g., case-control, case-cohort). This includes developing pipelines and software packages for molecular data pre-processing and quality control, performing GWAS, Mendelian randomisation, and colocalisation analyses. I supervise Master’s and PhD students across institutions and have experience in wet-lab settings, enabling me to bridge wet- and dry-lab approaches to advance translational research.
